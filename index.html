Transport in Melbourne City

Transport in Melbourne, the state capital of Victoria, Australia, consists of several interlinking modes.
Melbourne is a hub for intercity, intracity and regional travel. Road-based transport accounts for most trips across many parts of the city, facilitated by Australia's largest freeway network. Public transport, including the world's largest tram network, trains and buses, also forms a key part of the transport system.

Driving

There are over 342 kilometres of roads within the municipality of Melbourne. The majority of these roads, along with associated footpath, kerb and channel, drainage, and vehicular and pedestrian bridges, are the responsibility of the City of Melbourne.
